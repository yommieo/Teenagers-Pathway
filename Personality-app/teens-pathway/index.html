How to choose a career path as a teenager

Most of us has been through a series of very difficult or unpleasant experiences during high school years. The transition from a teenager to adult is exciting and terrifying, because no amount of “this will happen next” advice can prepare you for your own lived experiences. Here’s some encouragement: you don’t have to chart a course for your entire life! People change, and so do their careers. You just need to get started. And that means taking some time to examine or observe your mental and emotional processes to make sure the career in your head fit your personality or choose the right one. Taking the personality quiz for teens starts you on a path to greater self-knowledge that sets you on the pathway to massive positive impact on the society and success.

Personality Education in honor of Carl Jung

Carl Jung’s psychological type theory is a doorway to increased self-awareness and emotional intelligence. It helps you better comprehend and discuss how you see the world and how you interact with others.

Imagine a world where you are truly understood!




The writers at The School of Life have written quite a bit about the challenges and hopes of thinking about your place in the world:
There are so many things we already know without knowing that we know them – because we haven’t been trained in the art of gathering and interpreting our experiences. What is a beautiful city like? What is an ideal holiday? How does a good conversation flow? The questions may sound daunting, but we have answers to them already – for we all harbor, somewhere within our memories, recollections of well-being as we walked the streets of a capital, or felt our senses reopen in a new climate or registered our sympathies expanding at a table of friends.
